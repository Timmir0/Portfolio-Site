Artscapade

[Project Lead, Game Designer, Lead Programmer]

The capstone project that I worked with Team Artscapade where you play as a security guard whose goal is to defend a museum from thieves using security drones.

My responsibilies on this project were many. I took the brunt of a lot of the scripting needed to complete this project. That primarily means I was in charge of many of the more mechanical heavy parts when it comes to making this game. This includes the drones controls, our thieves AI, our map, and many other various parts.

The drone controls are rather simple all things considered. I have the drone follow a path of points created by the player drawing on the minimap. This in turn uses a line renderer that edits in real time as the player draws out the path. If the path ends up running into any obstacles the drone can't move pass, the path will automatically end as the drone heads in that direction. This ended up taking a lot of time debugging as new ways to cut paths through walls were discovered during playtesting, but the final iteration proved to be solid overall.

The next thing I was in charge of was the thief AI and placement within maps. The thieves themselves don't actually move through the museum in the traditional method. Instead they teleport from point to point to sell the illusion of the theives sneaking around, dodging the camera, and trying to be sneaky. For this, they have multiple different paths that they each are able to take, with different treasures that they are able to steal. Thus the importance to use the Security Drones pulse to send them back to step 1.

The map itself has notably went under a number of iterations, but the main purpose had remained the same. It allows the player to move the drones around while also chosing specific camera's they want to watch similar to games like FNAF. The biggest change during the many iteration of our game is changing the map from being a always present minimap on screen to giving it's own space. This meant shifting a lot of things around and experimenting, but this also allowed far smoother control of our drones, especially on bigger maps.

While the above is the brunt of my scripting work, I also did a lot of miscellaneous work with the rest of my group. A lot of which includes speaking to teammates on what needed to be done and what I can do to make it easier for them to get it done. There was also a decent amount of time spent on bug fixing, both my own and bugs caused by other group members.

Overall the project proved to be an enormous team effort that I believe we are all proud of.
